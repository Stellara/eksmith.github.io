Arrays and Hashes

Some of Ruby's Data Structures 101

January 11, 2015

Arrays and hashes are two of the most fundamental data structures in all of computer science. They both store collections of objects that can be accessed after their creation. They are also flexible structures and grow or shrink in size to fit the number of objects in them and they can hold a mixture of different types of objects.

Arrays and hashes are easy to create and use in Ruby and come with powerful built-in methods that will let you hit the ground running and start to do cool things with them very quickly.

When might you want to use one over the other?

Now, with a solid grasp of what arrays and hashes are, how you can create them, access them, and use them, you might wonder how you should know when to use one or the other.

Generally, you can create an array to do the same thing you would do with a hash, but for simplicity and readability you might choose a hash over an array if you need to associate something with something else.

Choose a hash if:

  • You need to relate your objects to each other
  • The order of your objects does not matter
  • You do not have rigid requirements for how to access your objects

Choose an array if:

  • You need a simple collection of objects
  • Their order matters
  • You need to be able to access them by integers

To wrap it all up...

Arrays and hashes are flexible data structures that are easy to create, access, and use. Ruby offers powerful built in methods for these classes and with these, you can write a wide variety of robust programs.
